About this week

This week I learnt all about line charts and axes, or at least the beginning of it. It sounds quite simple, but getting a line onto the screen and correctly mapping it to time and values actually involves quite a few different parts... One thing that started to click this week is that D3 builds visualisations piece by piece. Rather than thinking I want a line chart, you're working directly with SVG: Scalable Vector Graphics. Unlike a pixel-based image (where you're essentially colouring in a grid), SVG describes shapes mathematically: a line is just two points with a path between them, a circle is a centre point and a radius. Scale it up or scale it down, it’s really up to you to set the dimensions and style.

D3 leans into this fully. I’m not telling it "make me a line chart" and getting something back, unlike your traditional tools like Power BI or Tableau, or even coding languages like Python. You're constructing every part of the chart yourself: here’s a path, drive it with these values; here are some circles, place them at these coordinates. A traditional charting library would give me a line chart functional, but ultimately fixed with no design choices. D3 gives you the raw materials to build whatever you’re imagining.

She's Been Writing

female authors leading the charts on goodreads

Source: Goodreads — most read books in the UK over the last 12 months.

With International Women’s Day approaching, women dominate the current Goodreads charts, with female authors behind many of the books readers are adding to their shelves right now. The list spans genres, from fast-paced thrillers to expansive fantasy series.

A closer look at the rankings shows a clear romantasy takeover. We can’t help but notice Rebecca Yarros and Sarah J. Maas holding multiple spots at once: proof that readers are fully committed to dragons, fae politics, and emotional damage (the best kind). Maas has spoken candidly about the difficulty of writing these worlds, including on Call Her Daddy, where she connects the intensity of the work to her own therapeutic journey.
